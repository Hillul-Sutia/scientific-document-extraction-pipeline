Table 1: Fermented Food Master

Core schema:
| food_id | food_name | category | type | ethnic_group | source_pdf |

Definitions:
- category describes the food substrate/category, for example Fermented Soybean,
  Fermented Fish, Fermented Vegetable, Fermented Dairy, or Fermented Beverage.
- type describes product form, for example Solid Fermented Food, Liquid Fermented
  Food, Alcoholic Fermented Beverage, or Non-alcoholic Fermented Beverage.
- source_pdf is the first supporting PDF. source_pdfs contains every supporting PDF.

Example:
| F0001 | Axone | Fermented Soybean | Solid Fermented Food | Sumi | paper1.pdf |


Table 2: Raw Materials

Core schema:
| food_id | raw_material | amount | preparation_method |

Example:
| F0001 | Soybean | null | Boiled before fermentation |


Table 3: Geographic Distribution

Core schema:
| food_id | state | district | ethnic_group | village |

Example:
| F0001 | Nagaland | Zunheboto | Sumi | Akuluto |


Table 4: Nutrition (long format)

Core schema:
| food_id | category | parameter | value | unit |

Allowed category examples:
- Proximate Composition
- Mineral Composition
- Vitamin Composition
- Amino Acid Composition
- Fatty Acid Composition
- Energy Composition

Examples:
| F0001 | Proximate Composition | Moisture | 8.2 | % |
| F0001 | Proximate Composition | Protein | 22.4 | g/100g |
| F0001 | Mineral Composition | Calcium | 55 | mg/100g |


Table 5: Proximate Composition (wide format, derived from Table 4)

Core schema:
| food_id | entity_type | entity_name | moisture | ash | protein | fat | fiber | carbohydrate |

Example:
| F0001 | food | Axone | 8.2 % | 1.5 % | 22.1 g/100g | 12.2 g/100g | 5.5 g/100g | 50.2 g/100g |


Table 6: Microbiome

Core schema:
| food_id | taxonomy_level | taxonomy_name |

Allowed taxonomy levels:
- phylum
- class
- order
- family
- genus
- species

Examples:
| F0001 | genus | Lactobacillus |
| F0001 | species | Lactobacillus plantarum |


Table 7: Predominant Microbes

Core schema:
| food_id | microbe | count | source_pdf |

Definition:
- count preserves the exact abundance, percentage, CFU measurement, population,
  or other quantity reported by the paper. It is null when predominance is explicit
  but no quantitative count is supplied.

Example:
| F0001 | Lactobacillus plantarum | 8 log CFU/g | paper1.pdf |


Provenance fields

Extraction JSON records additionally contain:
- food_name
- source_pdf
- evidence_chunk_ids
- evidence: chunk_id, source_pdf, page_start, page_end, and section

Food-to-document occurrences are stored separately in food_occurrences.json.
